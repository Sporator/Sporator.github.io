Built for our climate

At Durango-area elevations the sun degrades roofing faster than it does at sea level, and snow load plus freeze-thaw can open up seams and flashing over a single winter. We spec materials and details for that reality, not for a catalog photo, so your roof holds up to real mountain and high-desert weather.

Frequently asked questions

How long should a roof last in this climate?

It depends on the material and exposure. A quality asphalt shingle roof commonly lasts 20 to 30 years here, while metal can go considerably longer. Altitude UV and snow load shorten the low end, which is why proper installation and ventilation matter so much.

Metal or shingle for a home up here?

Both work well when installed correctly. Metal sheds snow and lasts longer; shingles cost less up front and are easy to repair. We will look at your roof's pitch, exposure, and your budget and give you a straight recommendation.
